Energy Not Renewable

In Brazil, nuclear energy accounts for only about 1% of electricity generation. The process of developing the use of nuclear energy in our country began in the 1950s and proved to be lengthy and turbulent, with accusations of corruption and decades of construction stoppages.

South America has only 4 plants in operation, with Brazil holding two: Angra I and II, currently in the process of building Angra III. Eletronuclear manages Angra I and II, which together produce approximately 1.6 gigawatts of energy per hour, which represents approximately 4.8% of the total energy production in Brazil.

Petroleum energy is one of the main sources of Brazilian energy. The main oil basins are: Campos Basin, the largest in Brazil; Santos Basin, Espírito Santo Basin and Recôncavo Baiano Basin. A few years ago the country imported about 60% of the oil consumed domestically.
