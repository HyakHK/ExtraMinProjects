Nuclear Energy

Energy Not Renewable

The energy released by the nucleus of integrating atoms that is the action of their action. Uranium is commercially one of the main elements from which we can obtain this energy. The energy potential of uranium surpasses the possibilities of petroleum and other physical materials: approximately half a kilo of uranium is capable of providing as much energy as 1,360 tons of coal. Brazil already had the 6th largest uranium reserve in the world and the entire national territory has not yet been made

Uranium is transformed into pellets at the Nuclear Fuel Factory - FCN in Resende and then inserted into a zircaloy tube to form the fuel element. Two tablets are enough to generate energy for a month for a house with 4 people. Brazil dominates the entire nuclear energy cycle and FCN is already enriching the uranium pellet so that it can be used in our power plants.

The world currently has 440 nuclear plants and 23 more under construction. Europe has 207 nuclear plants and is building seven more. Japan, Korea and China together have 82 plants and North America (USA, Canada and Mexico) currently has 123 plants in operation
