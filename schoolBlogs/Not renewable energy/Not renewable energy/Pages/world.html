Natural gas

Energy Not Renewable

Natural gas is a fossil fuel that is found in nature, usually in deep underground reservoirs, associated or not with oil. Like oil, it results from the degradation of organic matter, fossils of animals and prehistoric plants, being removed from the earth through drilling.

With a cleaner burning than other fuels, natural gas is the result of the combination of gaseous hydrocarbons, under normal atmospheric conditions of pressure and temperature, containing mainly methane and ethane.

Before being distributed through pipelines, natural gas passes through a processing unit, in which impurities such as water, other gases and sand are removed, in addition to condensable and heavier components, such as natural gasoline and liquefied petroleum gas (LPG or “cooking gas”). Natural gas reserves in Brazil are vast and new reserves are being discovered, which guarantees supply throughout the Brazilian territory for a long time.
