Petroleum

Energy Not Renewable

Petroleum energy is obtained from fuels, which are its derivatives, which can be fuel oil, diesel oil and refinery gas. Diesel generators, for example, serve systems that are not covered by the conventional power grid.

Considering that in Brazil the main source of energy is hydraulic, thermal generation is essential to supply the demand in peak periods and to reach places that are not in the National Interconnected System (SIN).

Today, oil is the main source of energy in the world and is present in practically everything we use, directly or indirectly, since it is a raw material for countless derivatives and by-products, from fuel gas to plastics and rubbers.
