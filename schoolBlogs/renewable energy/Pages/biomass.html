Biomass

Posted on september 27, 2022 by Matheus mendes, Gabi Macedo, Arthur Alfaiate and Maria nyckolle
Energy Renewable

Biomass is a renewable energy source that uses organic elements, that is, with ample regeneration capacity, where energy production through biomass occurs mainly via pyrolysis, gasification, combustion and co- combustion procedures. Woody and non-woody vegetables, various organic residues and biofluids are sources of biomass that have the advantage of using renewable natural resources for energy production

Biomass production generates negative environmental impacts, such as the emission of pollutants, the occurrence of fires and the potentiation of deforestation, but it is also a source of energy for traditional use throughout the world, especially in countries such as China, Brazil and India. . In Brazil, biomass is used primarily for the generation of electricity and for the production of biofuels. Biomass-derived products include biodiesel, ethanol, biogas, methanol and biomethane.

Biomass operation

The most used methods for generating energy from biomass are arranged in two large groups. The first one, which involves pyrolysis and gasification processes, is linked to the thermochemical conversion of biomass inputs. These processes involve the adoption of heating techniques and are directly linked to the production of gases, in the case of gasification, and to the origination of solid, liquid and gaseous elements, in the case of pyrolysis.

he second most used method in energy production through biomass is combustion. In this procedure, the burning of biomass is carried out at high temperatures that heat the water of the plants, resulting in steam that is transformed into energy through the movement of the turbines. Furthermore, when this process is carried out together with the burning of another element, including a fossil, it is called co-combustion.
