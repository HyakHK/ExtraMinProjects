Hydroelectric power

Hydroelectric energy is that obtained by the force of the waters. This energy is produced by the use of the hydraulic potential, that is, the strength of the waters of the rivers, mediated by the construction of hydroelectric plants, those that will provide electricity to the population.

How Hydroeletric power works

Hydroelectric power is produced in hydroelectric plants according to the process of transformation of potential, kinetic and mechanical energy that, in turn, reaches the population in the form of electrical energy transported by the wires. In other words, the hydroelectric plant uses hydraulic energy for the production of electricity.

Advantages and Disadvantages of Hydroelectric Power

Despite being considered a renewable, clean energy (does not emit pollutants into the atmosphere), hydroelectric energy, since it needs the construction of hydroelectric plants to be produced, causes some environmental impacts, namely: flooding of areas, destruction of ecosystems (fauna, flora, soil), extinction of species, destruction of riverside, quilombola and indigenous communities. In this sense, it is important to note that for the construction of a hydroelectric plant, the determined places are far from urban centers, but close to many communities that live on land, hunting, fishing.

Offshore wind energy is that source of energy obtained by taking advantage of the force of the wind produced on the high seas, where it reaches a higher and more constant speed, due to the absence of barriers. In order to make the most of this resource, mega-structures are developed on the seabed and equipped with the latest technical innovations.

Hydroelectric Power in Brazil

It is important to note that in Brazil more than 90% of the electricity produced is supplied by hydroelectric plants since the country has a large number of caudal rivers and its hydraulic potential is the third best in the world, after Russia and China.
