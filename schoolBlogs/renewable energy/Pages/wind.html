Wind Energy

Posted on september 27, 2022 by Matheus mendes, Gabi Macedo, Arthur Alfaiate and Maria nyckolle
Energy Renewable

Wind energy is that obtained from the force of the wind, the extraction process is carried out mainly thanks to the rotor (which transforms kinetic energy into mechanical energy) and the generator (which transforms this mechanical energy into electrical energy

To take advantage of the kinetic energy of the wind and convert it into electrical energy, it is necessary to use a wind turbine. The best possible use of these giant generators depends on the strength of the wind. Therefore, wind farms, which group together a large number of wind turbines and make it possible to obtain this energy in large quantities, must be placed in places where the presence of the wind is predominant. These wind turbines are the mechanisms responsible for transforming kinematic energy into mechanical energy.

Types of wind energy

Currently, there are two types of wind energy depending on where the wind turbines are installed:

Onshore wind energy is responsible for producing electricity from the use of wind from wind farms located on land. For this, several wind turbines are installed capable of transforming the kinetic energy of the wind into electrical energy suitable for consumption and integrating it into the distribution network.

Offshore wind energy is that source of energy obtained by taking advantage of the force of the wind produced on the high seas, where it reaches a higher and more constant speed, due to the absence of barriers. In order to make the most of this resource, mega-structures are developed on the seabed and equipped with the latest technical innovations.
